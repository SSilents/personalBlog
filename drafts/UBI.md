# A case for Universal Basic Income

## Introduction

For me Universal Basic Income (UBI) seemed like a bit of a pipe dream; it seemed like it would be too expensive, too difficult to implement. My view started to change when I did a bit of research and had a look into what implementation of UBI would look like.

## What is UBI?

UBI is an alternative way to provide support to the less fortunate in society. Currently the system we have in place takes the form of Universal Credit and Pension Credit, or as most people know it: benefits. Universal Credit (UC) is dished out to those who are "on low income, out of work or [you] cannot work" according to the Government website. This means the Government picks and chooses who needs and who deserves benefits to support their living costs, this absent-by-default system means lots of time and money is put into sorting out who UC should be given to.

UBI on the other hand works as a present-by-default system and those who don't need the supplementary income will pay it back in tax.
